Boswa CIB

A complete guide to the Boswa Culinary Institute of Botswana's School Management & HR system — for students, lecturers, department heads, administrators, applicants and HR staff.

Published 15 July 2026 Covers Academic + HR modules Support Automate Africa
Chapter 1

Welcome & overview

Boswa CIB is the web-based system Boswa Culinary Institute of Botswana uses to run its academic and administrative operations — from a prospective student's first application, through classes, exams and transcripts, to staff payroll and time-and-attendance.

What it does

The system is split into two connected halves, both reached from the same sign-in screen:

  • Academic (School Management) — admissions, student records, classes and modules, timetables, attendance registers, exams and assignments, results, and official transcripts.
  • HR & Payroll — employee records, contracts, payslips, leave and loan requests, employee documents, and biometric clock-in attendance. Visible only to HR staff, managers, and the Super Admin.

How to access it

Boswa CIB runs entirely in your web browser — there is nothing to install. Open the system's web address, sign in with the email and password issued to you, and you'll land on a dashboard tailored to your role. No two staff members necessarily see the same menu: the sidebar and dashboard adapt automatically to what your role is permitted to do.

Chapter 3

Signing in & first steps

  1. Go to the Boswa CIB sign-in page. Enter the email and password given to you by your administrator, then select Sign In. Use the eye icon to reveal your password if you want to check it before submitting.
  2. If this is your very first sign-in, you'll be asked to set a new password before you can do anything else — this screen cannot be dismissed until you complete it.
  3. You'll land on your Dashboard, with a sidebar on the left scoped to your role (see Chapter 2) and a top bar showing the current academic year and semester.
  4. To sign out at any time, open the user menu at the bottom of the sidebar and select the sign-out icon.
Not a student or staff member yet? Use Apply for Admission on the sign-in screen instead of signing in — see Chapter 12.
Chapter 4

Finding your way around

Sidebar

Grouped into sections (Main, Academic, Operations, Management, HR Management, Self Service, …). Sections with sub-items expand when clicked. A red badge on a menu item — e.g. Leaves or Notifications — means something needs your attention, such as a pending approval.

Top bar

Shows a breadcrumb for the page you're on, the active academic year and semester, and a notification bell. If information looks out of date or a class doesn't appear, check that the correct year/semester is active (Configuration, admin only).

Working with records

Most sections follow the same pattern: a searchable/filterable list, an Add or + button that opens a pop-up form, and a confirmation message ("toast") in the corner once you save. Click a row to view or edit its details.

My Profile

Update your name, email and department, change your password, and — for students — upload a profile photo. Reached from your name at the bottom of the sidebar.

Chapter 5 · Admin / HOD / HOY / Lecturer

Student records

The Students page is the master directory of every enrolled student, scoped to what your role can see (a lecturer sees only students in their own classes; a HOD sees their department).

  • Search & filter by name, student ID, or class. A "missing enrolment date" filter flags incomplete records.
  • Open a student to see five tabs: Personal Details, Contact & Identity, Guardian / Next of Kin, Academic, and Marks.
  • Retakes — if a student fails a module, you can create a new attempt for a later year/semester directly from their Marks tab. The original attempt is kept on record and automatically marked "superseded" once the retake is graded — only the latest attempt counts toward their GPA.

Lecturers and Classes (admin/super admin) manage the teaching staff directory and class/cohort setup that everything else — timetables, attendance, exams — is built on.

Chapter 6 · Admin / HOD / HOY / Lecturer

Classes, modules, timetable & attendance

Modules & Module Mapping

Modules are subjects/units, each flagged as having a practical component or not — this flag controls which exam and mark types are available later. HODs use Module Mapping to link modules to programmes at the correct study year/semester.

Timetable

Class schedules, viewable by staff for the classes they teach, and by students as "My Timetable."

Marking Attendance

Open Attendance, choose a class, module and date, then pick which register you're taking:

  • Lesson Start — mark each student Present, Absent, or Late.
  • Lesson End — mark each student Present or Absent.

Everyone defaults to Present; click a student's status pill to change it, watch the live Present/Absent/Late counters update, then Save. Switch to the Summary tab for a class-level attendance report over time.

Chapter 7 · Admin / HOD / HOY / Lecturer

Exams, assignments, results & grades

Exams

Create an exam with a name, module, class, date, start/end time and room. The list of available exam types changes automatically: theory-only modules only offer Written/Final Theory/Oral Exam, while modules with a practical component also offer Practical/Recipe/Final Practical types.

Assignments

Create coursework with a title, description, module, class, due date, total marks, submission type, and an optional attachment (up to 10 MB) that students can download.

Results

View computed marks per student per module — coursework, practical, final exam, overall total and letter grade — using the same grading formula used on transcripts and reports.

Reports (HOD / HOY / Admin)

Class- and module-level summaries of marks and grade distribution, for tracking how a cohort is performing.

Chapter 8 · Admin / HOD / HOY / Student

Transcripts

The official academic transcript is the most formal document the system produces, and it's built to look and behave like one: a letterhead, a security watermark, and a real signature — not just a table of marks.

Viewing & printing a transcript

Staff open Transcripts, search for a student, and select View for an on-screen preview or Print to open the formatted document ready for Ctrl/Cmd+P or straight to a printer. Students see their own transcript automatically under My Transcript — no search needed.

What's on it

  • Header — the Boswa crest, institute name, and contact details (address, PO Box, phone, email, website).
  • Marks by semester — every module attempted, its mark and letter grade, credits, and a per-semester and overall GPA (credit-weighted). If a module was retaken, the earlier attempt shows struck through as superseded, and doesn't count toward the GPA.
  • Certification statement and a signature block — the signatory's name, title, and a genuine signature image.
  • Watermark — a faint Boswa monogram behind the text on every page, so photocopies and edits are harder to pass off as originals.
  • Letterhead footer — the institute's contact banner reprinted at the foot of the page.

Setting the signatory admin / super_admin only

From the button in the Transcripts page header, administrators can set who signs every transcript issued going forward:

  • Type the Issued By name and Position (e.g. "Deputy Principal").
  • Provide a signature either by drawing it directly on the signature pad (mouse, touch or stylus all work — use Clear to redo it) or by uploading an image of a signature.
  • Save. The new signatory and signature immediately appear on every transcript from then on.
Chapter 9 · Admin / Super Admin

Admissions & progression

Admissions

Every application submitted through the public Apply page (Chapter 12) lands here. Search or filter by status — submitted, under_review, accepted, rejected, awaiting_enrollment, enrolled — and open one to review the applicant's details, programme choices, and uploaded documents (ID and qualification). Opening an application automatically moves it to "Under Review."

  • Accept — optionally add a note; the applicant is notified and an offer letter is generated for them to download.
  • Reject — record a reason, which the applicant will see on their status page.

Progression

At the end of a semester, select students by class and approve or reject their promotion to the next year/semester. The system checks that marks have been recorded and that no module is failing before it will let you approve a promotion.

Chapter 10 · Admin / Super Admin

Configuration & user accounts

Configuration

Five tabs govern the whole academic structure:

  • Programmes — name, type (Diploma/Certificate/Degree), NQF level, years, semesters per year, and intake months.
  • Departments, Modules, Mapping and Rooms — the building blocks everything else (classes, timetables, exams) draws from.

This is also where the current academic year and semester are set — the value shown in the top bar everywhere in the system.

User Management

A combined directory of every login account — staff and students. Search, filter by staff/student, reset a password, or delete an account. Deleting is permanent and cannot be undone, so it always asks for confirmation first.

Notifications

Post announcements that appear as a banner on every user's dashboard, and manage the notification bell feed.

Photo Gallery

Upload and organise student and event photos into folders. Students only ever see their own photos; staff can browse and download whole folders as a ZIP.

Chapter 11 · Student

For students

A student's sidebar is intentionally short — just what you need to follow your own studies.

  • My Dashboard — your class, programme, current year/semester, module count, attendance percentage, and upcoming assignments at a glance.
  • My Modules, My Timetable, Notes — what you're studying, when, and shared course materials.
  • Assignments — coursework set for your class, with due dates and any attached files.
  • My Results — your marks and grade for every module once your lecturer has entered them.
  • My Transcript — your official academic record, viewable and printable at any time (Chapter 8).
  • My Profile — change your password or update your profile photo.
Chapter 12 · Public

Applying for admission

Browsing programmes & applying

Anyone can visit the Apply page (linked from the sign-in screen) without an account. Browse the list of programmes, expand one to see the modules it covers by year and semester, then select Apply Now on your first-choice programme.

The registration form collects your account details, personal information, guardian/next-of-kin details, your programme choices and intake, and uploads of your ID and qualification documents. You'll be asked to tick a declaration before submitting.

Tracking your application

After submitting, sign in with the account you just created to see your personal Applicant Portal, showing a progress tracker: Submitted → Under Review → Accepted → Enrolled (or Rejected, with a reason shown).

Once accepted, you can download your offer letter and select Accept Offer & Submit Sponsorship to provide how your studies will be funded — the final step before enrolment.

Chapter 13 · Super Admin / HR / Manager

HR & payroll management

A separate "HR Management" section of the sidebar, visible only to HR staff, the Super Admin, and — in mostly read/approval form — Managers.

Employees

Staff directory and individual employee files, organised into departments and employee groups.

Payroll

Payslips (search, filter by pay period/status, confirm or delete), a detailed payslip breakdown with a downloadable PDF, batch generation for a whole pay run, a Payroll Report, and Pay Components to define salary elements.

Contracts

Employee contracts (wage, start date, status) built from reusable Contract Templates.

Leaves

Employees' leave balances and requests, with a multi-stage approval workflow — larger requests can automatically escalate to a higher approver. Configure Leave Types and pull a Leave Report.

Loans / Advances

Salary advance and loan requests and approvals, Loan Types, and a Loan Report.

Documents

Store employee documents with automatic expiry alerts — configure which document types need expiry tracking in Document Settings.

Super Admin only: Workflow Management (design the approval stages for Leave/Loan/general requests and who owns each stage), Employee Groups, and HR User Management.
Chapter 14 · Employee / Manager / Lecturer / HOD / HOY / HR

Employee self-service

  • My File — your own employee record.
  • My Payslips — download your own payslips.
  • My Leaves — apply for leave, track a request through each approval stage, and check your balance and a leave calendar.
  • My Loans — apply for a salary advance or loan and track its status.
Chapter 15 · HR / Super Admin

Biometric attendance

Staff clock-in/out is captured by a biometric terminal (face, card or fingerprint) at the Boswa campus — separate from the academic class-attendance registers in Chapter 6.

  • Live Dashboard — most recent punches as they arrive (synced roughly every 5 minutes).
  • Attendance Report — summarised staff attendance over a date range.
  • Raw Punches — every individual clock event, for auditing.
  • Devices & Settings — configured work-start times, grace periods, and Saturday rules.

This page only ever displays what has already synced from the terminal — if a staff member's punches aren't appearing, it usually means the device hasn't reported them yet, or their Person ID isn't set up on the terminal. Contact your system administrator if data looks missing for more than a few minutes.

Chapter 16

Tips & troubleshooting

A class or student is missing

Check that the correct academic year and semester are active — most academic lists (like the Dashboard's "Active Classes") only show records for the currently selected period. An administrator sets this in Configuration.

I forgot my password

You can't reset your own password from the sign-in screen. Ask an administrator to reset it for you from User Management (academic side) or HR User Management (HR side) — you'll be prompted to set a new one on your next sign-in.

A student can't see their marks

Marks only appear once a lecturer has entered them for that module. If a mark is missing entirely, ask the module's lecturer to check the Results/Marks entry for that student.

The transcript signature looks wrong

The signature and "Issued By" details are set once, centrally, and apply to every transcript from then on — see Chapter 8. Only admin/super_admin can change them.

Still stuck? Boswa CIB is maintained by Automate Africa. For anything this manual doesn't cover, contact your school administrator, who can escalate to the support team.
